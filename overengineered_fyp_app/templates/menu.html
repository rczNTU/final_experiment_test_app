FYP EEG Study

Choose and Order Patterns




Instructions

This study investigates how the brain responds to visual flicker patterns while EEG signals are recorded.

The experiment consists of 20 trials: 10 baseline trials and 10 flicker-pattern trials. Each trial lasts around 20 seconds, so the total duration of the experiment is approximately 400 seconds (6 minutes and 40 seconds).

During the experiment you will see a flickering visual stimulus on the screen, which may appear as small flashing lights or "firefly-like" patterns. Please keep your eyes focused on the centre of the screen at all times and avoid unnecessary movement. Try to minimise blinking if possible.

Occasionally, the fixation cross in the centre of the screen will briefly shrink into a small box. When you notice this change, press the SPACEBAR.

Since the experiment involves repeating several trials, it may feel a little repetitive at times. This small reaction-time task is included to help keep you attentive while EEG data is being recorded.
